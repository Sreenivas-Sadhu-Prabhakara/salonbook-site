The leak nobody measures

You are running a rebooking business.
Your diary just can’t see it.

A salon lives or dies on people coming back. But the way most owners run it, the coming-back part is left to luck.

📓

The diary forgets

A paper register tells you who came yesterday. It never tells you who was supposed to come this week and didn’t.

💬

WhatsApp is a mess

Reminders live in your head and a hundred chat threads. The client who last came 6 weeks ago quietly slips away — and you never notice.

🎟️

Packages leak money

You sold a 10-session package. How many sessions are left? Nobody’s sure — so staff give away sessions you already spent.

Every regular who forgets to rebook is revenue you already earned, walking out the door in someone else’s hands.

The wedge

A salon is a cadence business.

A haircut is due again in 4 weeks. A root touch-up in 3. A spa facial every 6. The whole game is one nudge — “you’re due for your next visit” — sent to the right person on the right day.

Get the cadence right and the chair fills itself. SalonBook does one thing obsessively well: it knows every client’s rhythm and never lets it break.

4–6 wks the window where a regular either rebooks with you — or becomes someone else’s regular.

How it works

Four steps. Then the book runs the book.

  1. 1

    Log the visit

    Mark the appointment done in the day view. Pick the service — say “Global Colour”.

  2. 2

    We set the due date

    Each service carries its own rebook window. Last visit + that window = the exact day this client is due back.

  3. 3

    The due list surfaces

    Every morning, SalonBook shows who’s due today, who’s overdue, and who’s coming up — sorted by urgency.

  4. 4

    One tap to nudge

    Send a warm, ready-written reminder to the outbox. They rebook. The chair stays full.

Common questions

Do my clients need to install anything?

No. You run SalonBook; nudges reach clients on plain WhatsApp and SMS. They just get a friendly reminder and reply to rebook.

I already have everything in a paper diary. Is switching a headache?

Start fresh from today — no migration needed. Log visits as they happen and within a few weeks the due list fills itself. Most owners are running by day two.

How does it decide when someone is “due”?

Every service has a default rebook window — a haircut around 4 weeks, colour around 3, a facial around 6. Last visit plus that window is the due date. You can tweak any window to match your salon.
